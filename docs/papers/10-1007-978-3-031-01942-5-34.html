Performance Evaluation of Pilot-Based Channel Estimation for Conventional, Cell-Free, and Small-Cell Massive MIMO Networks

Keywords: 6G Wireless communication systems, Massive MIMO, Channel estimation, Conventional Massive MIMO network, Cell-Free, Small-Cell

Massive multiple-input multiple-output (M-MIMO) and net- work design are two contemporary technologies that have received a lot of attention. Moreover, an innovative network design known as cell-free (CF) has attracted considerable interest. Thus, addressing and assessing the channel estimation (CE) operation in a CF M-MIMO network is an appealing topic. Consequently, this paper focuses on the M-MIMO technology, in particular, the CE operation for three differents three-time division duplex-enabled M-MIMO networks, namely, the conventional M- MIMO network, the CF M-MIMO network, and the small-cell M-MIMO network. Furthermore, for each network design, we apply the minimum mean square error (MMSE) estimator to evaluate and determine the CE operation, including the expression of the large-scale fading model. On the other hand, we established the normalized mean square error formula for the MMSE estimator regarding each network configuration. Addition- ally, a pilot assignment scheme is proposed for CF M-MIMO network. Numerical results are displayed to confirm our theoretical expressions.
