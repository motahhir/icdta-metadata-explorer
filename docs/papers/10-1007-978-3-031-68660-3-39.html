Keywords: Energy Efficiency, Digital Economy, Top Management

In today's ever-changing business environment, the call for energy efficiency has never been more urgent. Companies worldwide are under growing pressure to optimize resource utilization, reduce environmental impact, and improve operational performance. However, despite increasing awareness of the benefits of sustainable practices, many organizations struggle to turn their intentions into concrete actions. This perspective article delves into the intricate connections between top management, digital transformation, and energy efficiency within companies, aiming to address this challenge. Through an analysis of pertinent literature and empirical evidence, we examine the role of top management and digital transformation in driving energy efficiency initiatives and explore the mechanisms through which organizations can optimize energy efficiency. By capturing these interconnections, this perspective article offers policy recommendations for advancing top management in the service of digital transformation, ultimately bridging the gap between intention and action in implementing energy efficiency initiatives within organizations.
