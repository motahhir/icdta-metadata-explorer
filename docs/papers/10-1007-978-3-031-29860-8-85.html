Smooth Collision-Free Trajectory Planner for AMR Based on B-spline Interpolation and I-GWO

Keywords: Trajectory planning, Obstacle avoidance, AMR, B-spline, Meta-heuristic algorithms, I-GWO

The problem of trajectory planning is one of the important tasks of autonomous mobile robots (AMR). For that, in this paper, a trajectory planning algorithm is developed to generate a smooth trajectory for AMR in a static environment. The generated trajectory links a starting point with an end point while avoiding static obstacles. The trajectory curve is parameterized with the B-spline interpolation due to its smoothness properties and to reduce the number of points needed to define. Therefore, to find the optimal B-spline parameters, the trajectory planning problem has turned into an optimization problem. Improved Grey Wolf Optimizer (I-GWO) was selected as optimization algorithm because of its excellent optimization performance and global search capability. Different I-GWO settings were tested and the best selected setting was compared with other optimization algorithms. The results show that the selected optimization algorithm is more efficient and accurate.
