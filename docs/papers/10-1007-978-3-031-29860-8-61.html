Energy Efficiency Model-Based Predictive Maintenance for Induction Motor Fault Prediction Using Digital Twin Concept

Keywords: Predictive maintenance, Broken rotor bar detection, Induction Motor, Digital twin, Energy efficiency

The uninterrupted and faultless operation of electrical machines has become a myth and at the same time a key goal for modern industry. Preventing and predicting induction motors (IMs) failures and shutdowns is important to guarantee the functionality of industrial loads, and allow to reduce considerably the maintenance cost. This paper proposes a new maintenance method based on energy efficiency by using the digital twin (DT) concept and IM losses as condition indicators for broken rotor bar fault detection. In this method, all the IM losses are linked to the machine faults, and based on the losses values, the faults are predicted. The power of the DT model is harnessed and used optimally for loss-based fault detection and prediction. A dataset of 5 motors, consisting of 1 without defaults and 4 defective ones, are used to validate the proposed method. The results show that the proposed Energy efficiency-based predictive maintenance (PM) detects easily the fault related to the broken rotor, notifies about the machine’s state of health, and can be integrated as a service for the Digital Twin application.
