Keywords: Power system stability, Grid inertia, Frequency stability

Electricity generation has traditionally been dominated by synchronous generators connected to the high-voltage grid at specific points; this is known as a centralized generation. Transmission and distribution systems, on the other hand, have been built to ensure a one-way flow of electricity from generation to consumption. Today, environmental concerns and sustainable development are driving the replacement of traditional fossil fuel-based power plants with renewable energy sources, particularly wind and solar power plants, resulting in a significant change in the dynamics of the power system. This change is mainly due to the fact that the majority of renewable energy sources are connected through a power electronic interface, which affects the frequency dynamics and operating stability of the power system. The frequency dynamics are stronger in power systems with limited synchronous generation, making it more difficult to control the frequency and operate the power system. The influence of reduced rotational inertia on the stability of the power system is studied in this research. In the final phase, an ETAP 19.0.1 simulation will be run to investigate the effects of the power system inertia on the frequency response.
