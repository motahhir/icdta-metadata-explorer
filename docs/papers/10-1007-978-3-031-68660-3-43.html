Keywords: State of health, Electric vehicle, Remaining useful life, Deep learning

The degradation of lithium-ion batteries is a key area of research, as these batteries are widely used in many sectors, including electric vehicles, electronic devices, and renewable energies. Deep learning (DL) based evaluation systems have become widely adopted because of their capacity to process complex data and deliver accurate predictions. Lithium-ion batteries are subject to degradation over time, limiting their ability to store and deliver energy reliably. To monitor and manage the degradation of Li-ion cells effectively, research is now focusing on the use of evaluation systems based on DL algorithms like recurrent neural networks (RNN), convolutional neural networks (CNN), and hybrid models. These approaches make it possible to predict the remaining useful life (RUL) and the state of health (SoH) of the battery and to optimize its use to prolong its lifespan. This paper presents several techniques that can be used to predict RUL and SoH to evaluate battery degradation. It also provides an overview of the data collection process, as well as the challenges faced and solutions proposed.
