Application of Superconducting Magnet Energy Storage to Improve DFIG Behavior Under Sag Voltage

Keywords: Doubly fed induction generator (DFIG), Wind Turbine, Power fluctuation, voltage dip, Superconducting magnet energy storage (SMES)

In this research, we propose a new low-voltage control method (LVRT) to support the behavior of the double-fed induction generator (DFIG) under voltage dips or sudden changes in wind speed. To support the operation of the DFIG, protect the power converters and smooth the fluctuations of the output power of the DFIG. A superconducting Magnetic Energy Storage (SMES) scheme and its control are proposed. This method allows having a better faster response, to dampen the DC bus voltage variation and to reduce the wear and tear of the storage system. With this control, the battery wear is reduced and the intermediate circuit voltage is quickly regulated. The simulations in the MATLAB environment showed the advantages of the proposed strategy.
