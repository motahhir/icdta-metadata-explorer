Ecological Hammam in Fez: Design of a Combined Biomass and Solar Thermal Energy System

Keywords: Traditional hammam, Solar thermal, Heating floor, Hot water, Domestic hot water, environmental study, economic study, T-Sol simulation

This study was carried out within the framework of the development of “Ecological Hammams” aiming to improve the energy efficiency of traditional hammams in Morocco. To evaluate the energy performance, a comparative study on the economic and environmental importance was made for our combined system between solar thermal with flat plate collectors and biomass using a biomass boiler that consumes olive pomace as a biofuel for the heating of the hammam premises and the production of hot water for a public bath in the climatic conditions of Fez, Morocco. However, these systems are not yet widespread in Morocco, as traditional wood-fired boilers are still widely used as an energy source in various applications. Therefore, hammams consume huge amounts of wood that threaten forest areas and green fields with the release of huge amounts of CO2 and greenhouse gases that increase global warming. The use of the combined system avoids a large amount of CO2 emissions into the atmosphere in the order of 357,85 tons per year. Furthermore, our case study in this paper presents an efficient and excellent solution. Therefore, the integration of such a combined solar thermal system with a total coverage rate of approximately 42,4% and biomass will lead to energy and financial savings in consumption as well as a reduction in CO2 emissions.
