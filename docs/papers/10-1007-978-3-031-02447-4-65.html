Keywords: Prediction, Renewable energy, Extreme Learning Machine, Photovoltaic energy, Particle Swarm Optimization

Due to the many challenges that energy is currently witnessing, it has become necessary to rely on unlimited renewable energies such as solar ones. However, its discontinuous nature forces us to predict the power delivered by the photovoltaic generators. To deal with this issue, this paper exploits the major advantages of the algorithm Extreme Learning Machine and so proposes a model for predicting generated solar power. The Particle Swarm Optimization method is integrated to calculate the adequate number of hidden neurons. The goal of our method is to control the amount of energy generated to deal with the uncertainties of the system and to ensure the availability of energy supplies. The proposed method is to sense the average hourly photovoltaic energy using the temperature, radiation, and wind speed of the previous hour. The results obtained showed that this presented method is much better than related ones reported in the literature concerning prediction accuracy and time convergence.
