Impact of Integrating Digital Educational Resources on Student Motivation in Learning Nuclear Transformations

Keywords: Digital educational resources, Nuclear physics, Cognitive competition

The objective of this work is to measure the impact of the learning of physical sciences in the axis of nuclear transformations in the qualifying secondary school via the digital pedagogical resource “eduMedia-Lycee-Annee2-Physics Chemistry” in order to motivate the students of the international baccalaureate - French, physics option, all while being attentive because the experiments in this axis are dangerous, in particular in practical work. Thus, the use of digital resources can create cognitive competitive situations for students who want to reconstruct their own knowledge about fundamental notions and concepts in nuclear physics, such as the atomic nucleus, radioactivity, stability and instability of nuclei, radioactive decay, binding energy, fission and fusion, energy balance of a nuclear reaction. The methodology adopted consists in establishing a formative evaluation for both classes (group1 and group2), with the aim of analyzing the responses collected. The results obtained show that group 1, with the digital educational resource, obtained a better result in learning the concepts of nuclear physics: group 1 benefited from the digital methodology while group 2 was limited to the traditional methodology based only on the subject theory and some verbal explanation. Following the evaluation test, it was found that the attention of the students showed a distinguished motivation compared to that of the students of group2.
