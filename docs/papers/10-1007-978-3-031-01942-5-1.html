Keywords: Lean six sigma, Industry 4.0, Lean, Six Sigma, Digitalization, Literature review

Recently, Industry 4.0 “I4.0” has been identified as a priority research area and has attracted the interest of many researchers, universities and companies worldwide. I4.0 is now revolutionizing manufacturing operations management among other areas through the increased availability and accessibility of data. The capability of digital technologies for speed data collection and analysis promises a new age of continuous improvement and process performance. Lean six sigma “LSS”, the widely best continuous improvement concept used in manufacturing will be affected by I4.0 technologies. However, the relationship between the two paradigms is not well developed in the literature and remains to be studied. To fill this gap, this paper aims to address what has already been described in the literature and analyze the scientific research trends related to the combination of I4 and LSS in manufacturing context through a structured literature review. The findings confirm that Lean Six Sigma and Industry 4.0 is a new emerging research field that has attracted a great scientific interest over the last decade. Our study will relevant to both academics and practitioners, as it describes the main lines of research in the field. Further studies are needed to validate these results.
