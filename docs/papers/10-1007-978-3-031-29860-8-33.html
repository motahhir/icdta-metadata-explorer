Keywords: Higher Education, Quality Education, Remote Learning, Emergency Remote Teaching, Digitalization, ICTs, Moroccan Universities

During the COVID-19 pandemic wave, many entities concerned with the matter, notably the Moroccan government, hasted toward finding new alternatives to ensure the pedagogical continuity and resilience of academic institutions using the digitalization of content to ensure the transition from a face-to-face approach to virtual learning. This paper aims to evaluate the measures undertaken by the Moroccan government during the COVID-19 pandemic to ensure the ongoing learning process. A study that was exploratory in nature was adopted for this enquiry. To draw out conclusions and come up with recommendations, 12 universities were subject to this study, and 2 questionnaires were elaborated and addressed to both students and professors. The data collected were then analyzed and apprehended into graphs to obtain a better understanding of the situation. The results revealed that Moroccan universities were not yet ready for the 100% E-learning shift but were rather able to ensure more of a hybrid learning environment for their students.
