Keywords: CSP, Mirror, Soiling effect, Eastern Morocco

In this work, the optical loss that occurs specifically due to the soiling effect will be emphasized. Specifically, we will study the optical performance of CSP technologies under the climatic conditions of the city of Oujda (34.64968°N; –1.90°E). A sample mirror with a size of 150 × 150 × 3 mm3 used in CSP plants was installed in the climatic conditions of the city of Oujda to examine its optical efficiency, the daily variation of reflectivity is measured in two different cases, with regular cleaning (once a week) and without cleaning. The results showed that the energy production of the plant without cleaning can be reduced with a maximum rate of 80% during the summer period due to the high amount of aerosol particles, while for the case of regular cleaning, it showed that the losses are not as important as without cleaning, in addition, it can keep the rate of losses constant and at reasonable levels.
