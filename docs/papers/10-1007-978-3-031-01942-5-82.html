Keywords: DFIM, DTC, MPC, PTC

The aim of the study presented in this paper is to improve the performance of the direct torque control (DTC) applied to doubly fed induction motors (DFIM). The DTC is characterized by good speed tracking and high robustness against parametric variations of the motor and external disturbances. However, there are significant drawbacks to this method as well. On the one hand, the switching frequency is not controlled, with a high ripple in the stator flux and the torque. Therefore, a new approach is developed to improve the performance of DTC. Predictive torque control (PTC) is a technique that determines the appropriate voltage vector to apply in the next sampling period by constructing a unique cost function from a linear combination of objective functions. The torque and flux errors are accounted for utilising weight factors in a cost function. Finally, the proposed control is compared to DTC by simulation in the Matlab\Simulink environment.
