Design of a Non-inverting Buck-Boost Converter Controlled by Voltage-Mode PWM in TSMC 180 nm CMOS Technology

Keywords: Battery management system, Non-inverting Buck-Boost converter, Solar energy, Voltage-mode PWM control

This paper presents a design of a DC-DC non-inverting Buck-Boost converter controlled by a voltage-mode pulse width modulation (PWM) in TSMC 180 nm CMOS technology for a solar energy management system. Whatever the weather conditions or the sunlight incident angle, the presented power converter delivers to the load, a regulated DC voltage. Therefore, this system converts the input voltage into higher or lower voltages. Since the conventional Buck-Boost converter does not serve the needs of applications where a positive polarity is required. A four switches topology is implemented in this work to keep the polarity unchanged. A voltage-mode PWM control ensures the output voltage regulation by generating the appropriate PWM signal duty cycle following the input voltage value. The designed power converter delivers an output voltage in the range of [423.2—526.38 mV] for different input voltages ranging from 200 to 800 mV. The output current range is [41.92—52.43 mA]. The efficiency of the presented work ranges from 70.99 to 83.08%. The design area is 0.043 mm2.
