The Design of an EEG Bio-Amplifier for the Primary Motor Cortex Zone in 180 nm CMOS Technology

Keywords: EEG, AOP, CMOS, CMRR, IRN, The primary motor cortex

This article presents an amplification circuit for Electroencephalogram (EEG) signals related to the intention of movements in the primary motor cortex. The proposed design consists of a differential amplifier designed from an operational amplifier in 180 nm CMOS technology. It amplifies signals at low amplitudes 20 µV and includes a protection circuit as well as a bandpass filter making it possible to target the frequencies associated with the signal of interest. The system offers a differential gain of 60 dB, a Common Mode Rejection Ratio (CMRR) of 140 dB, and the equivalent noise referred to its input (IRN) is 96 nV at 14 Hz.
