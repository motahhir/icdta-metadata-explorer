Keywords: Machine learning, Deep learning, Autoencoders, Cancer, miRNA, RPPA, TCGA

Genomics and proteomics activities are the fundamental controllers of the living processes of any organism. They carry out most of the work, perform most life functions, and make up the majority of cellular structures. Moreover, their abnormal behaviors are the driving factors of many genetic diseases. Therefore, understanding these activities has become a fundamental mission of large-scale genomics research. However, this study field has some limitations, such as the sensitivity, the high dimensional, and the large scale of data. That is why it is a challenging application area of deep learning. However, this last is more effective when one wants to make sense of large and complex data sets. This paper uses a deep learning method based on autoencoders to solve a classification problem, which is cancer detection using two distinct annotated data sets: the microRNA data set and the reverse phase proteins array. The results were promising and proved that our deep integrative model was superior in the Cancer Type and the PFI score classification and prediction with high accuracy.
