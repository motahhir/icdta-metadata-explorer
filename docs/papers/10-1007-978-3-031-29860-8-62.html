Keywords: Hydrogen internal combustion engine, Fuel cell electric vehicle, Hydrogen generation, Renewable energy

The promotion of the adoption of energy resources other than fossil fuels is essential to meet the environmental requirement. Currently, the transportation sector is heavily dependent on petrol. Its transition to a fully electrified transportation sector is necessary, but yet, very challenging to accomplish since its adoption must be matched with an important upgrade in the electric power utility, an important infrastructure of recharging stations, and a dramatic shift in people’s discernment about electric vehicles. It is the authors’ opinion that hydrogen internal combustion engine vehicles (HICEV) constitute a reasonable middle step to transit towards fully electrified vehicles, in order to mitigate the pollution generated by gasoline-powered internal combustion engine vehicles (ICEV), the dependence on petrol in the transportation sector, and the negative impact on public health of ICEV’s in metropolitan areas. Furthermore, the case of generating hydrogen from solar energy through photovoltaic energy conversion is studied in the Moroccan context.
