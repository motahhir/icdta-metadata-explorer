Current Situation of Green Building Development in Morocco: Standards and Certification Systems

Keywords: Green building, Standards, Certification systems, Developing countries

The development of green building has emerged as a global trend to reduce the ecological footprint and well-being of societies, but in developing countries, the concept is still emerging. In such circumstances, the establishment of a national green building standard would be an important first step. Morocco has made significant progress in establishing national standards for environmental protection and assessment. However, this progress is not specifically applied to a sustainable policy in the construction sector. The development of green building in morocco is in its beginning phase, which currently lacks governmental and voluntary support to elaborate appropriate standards and regulations for green building. This article analyses the situation and progress of green standardization in the construction industry and the certification. In this context, Morocco’s green buildings are certified with 51 projects evaluated by HQE, LEED, and BREEAM. The findings of this paper indicate the national regulation is not specific to the construction industry except for the energy part of the thermal regulation. A framework for developing specific regulations for sustainability categories inspired by international systems was proposed that can help decision-makers adopt strategies to stimulate the development of green buildings in Morocco.
