Keywords: GPV, PV modeling, Solar water pumping system, DC Motor

Significant progress has been made recently in the field of photovoltaic (PV) generator conversion, with an emphasis on the use of multiple approaches. This has largely focused attention on irrigation fields, prompting several researchers to investigate ways to make photovoltaic installations more cost-effective and affordable. Additionally, several strategies for improving photovoltaic cell efficiency are considered, which are modeled at the level of increasing cell lifetime or extracting maximum power from the cells. With this in mind, the current study analyzes the various parameters that affect the manufacturing of GPVs, including their electrical characteristics. Additionally, this analysis is numerically based. A direct coupling is established between the GPV, which is composed of REC 330NP photovoltaic panels, and a solar water pumping system powered by a SQF 0–6-2 motor pump. The numerical results of the proposed analysis are presented to emphasize the critical nature of a matching converter that maximizes the extraction of power generated by the GPV.
