Keywords: Double MRT thermal lattice Boltzmann method, Flow control, Drag coefficient reduction, Heat exchange, Partitions control

In this research, a numerical investigation of the fluid flow and thermal convection phenomena around a heated square cylinder controlled by three attached downstream partitions is conducted. The numerical simulations are performed at a fixed Reynolds number (Re = 150) by using the double lattice Boltzmann method with multiple relaxation times (MRT-DLBM). The insertion of the partitions is to control the vortex shedding (reduction of the drag coefficient) and improve the heat transfer around the squares cylinder. The numerical results are presented as contours of the streamlines, the isotherms lines and the time and average drag coefficient. These results indicate that there is a critical length of the control partitions Lp = 4 where a maximum percentage reduction of the drag coefficient reached 17.33%. Also, regular and important heat exchange is observed for this crucial length. The numerical code has been validated with existing numerical investigations in the literature for a flow around a square cylinder without and with a single detached partition.
