Optimisation and Performance Evaluation of a Standalone Renewable Energy System in Congo-Brazzaville

Keywords: Optimisation, Renewable energy system, Standalone system, Congo-Brazzaville

Renewable energies have gained considerable interest in recent decades. The optimal use of renewable energies makes it possible to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and produce sustainable energy in various locations. This work assesses the techno-financial feasibility of an off-grid power system to meet the annual load of a residential dwelling in Congo Brazzaville. The HOMER program has been utilised to determine the optimal size of power generation systems. Moreover, a financial analysis has been performed for all scenarios to demonstrate the attractiveness of the proposed systems. The findings indicate that the PV/battery configuration is more efficient and financially viable than the hybrid PV/wind/battery and the standalone wind/battery configurations. For instance, the optimal system resulted in a net present cost estimated at 72.45% and 41.29% less than that of the wind/battery and PV/WT/battery configurations, respectively.
