Keywords: ROS, Gazebo world, Turtlebot Kobuki, Waypoint guidance, LQI control, Trajectory tracking

In view of computational challenges that may span autonomous navigation of mobile robots, mainly due to its complex structure, a low cost, simple structure scheme is proposed and verified under Gazebo in a ROS-based nonholonomic mobile robot “Turtlebot Kobuki”. Relying on LQI control strategy, the algorithm ensures the task of steering the robot through a set of waypoints allowing high performance and accurate path tracking. Simulating a virtual robot under Gazebo, experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed scheme.
