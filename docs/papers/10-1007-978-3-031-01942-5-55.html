Keywords: Switch mode power supply, Flyback converter, SPS, Pulse Width Modulation (PWM)

Power supply faces many challenges, they must meet stability, accuracy, efficiency and cost one of the best power supplies that meets all of these constraints is the switch mode power supply is a technique that continuously switches between on and off states to manage power consumption and reduce wasted energy. A flyback converter is a BUCK-BOOST converter that can raise or lower the voltage. It has an electrical isolation between the input and the output by means of an inductor coupled or split to form a transformer. In the first phase the switch is closed, the magnetic flux stores energy and in the second phase the switch is open, the energy stored in the transformer is transferred to the load the magnetic flux drops, which causes the diode to be forward biased it fires to transfer energy to the output and allows current to flow from the transformer and charge the capacitor and the load. This paper describes the design and testing of a stand-alone power supply control device for a flyback converter. The flyback controller was designed, simulated and implemented in Cadence using TSMC’s 180 nm SOI technology, with an input of 170 V and an output of 12.5 V, an efficiency of 91.7% and an optimized area of 0.0156 mm2.
