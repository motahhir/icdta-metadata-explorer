Keywords: Word Embedding, Word2Vec, Arabic Language, Morphology, NLP

The choice of a good word representation is often essential to carry out automatic processing tasks of written or spoken natural language. The “bag-of-words” representation is the most commonly used, where documents are represented by the frequency vectors of the words that compose them. However, this representation has the drawback of capturing very little information about the structure of the documents and their relationship to each other. For this reason, approaches based on n-grams have been introduced. More recently, methods based on artificial neural networks such as Word2Vec, GloVe and BERT have become popular. These methods are able to represent a word in the form of real-valued vectors with controlled size. This vector corresponds to the position of the word in a multi-dimensional space. This type of technique is called Word embedding. This paper proposes an original method based on AL-khalil Morpho Sys to generate morphological word embeddings. The results show that the information carried by the new vector allows to obtain relevant results for a concrete application such as Part of speech tagging.
