Inter-organizational Information System and Efficiency of the Tourism Supply Chain

Keywords: Supply chain, Supply chain tourism, Interorganizational information system, Logistics efficiency

In recent years, the relationship between logistics efficiency and the use of information systems has been studied conceptually and empirically. This relationship has become relevant with the advent of new information and communication technologies. Through this article, we aim to explore the links between the use of interorganizational information systems and the efficiency of the tourism supply chain. Our research approach is qualitative following an exploratory inductive logic, and the method is that of Delphi. The first finding is the absence of an interorganizational information system that facilitates logistics flows between supply chain actors. The second is that the experts (professionals) of the supply chain have agreed on the positive role of the information system in improving the efficiency of the supply chain. Thus, the actors of the tourism supply chain must invest more in new technologies by structuring an information system to control logistics flows, make the supply chain efficient and offer a successful global experience to tourists.
