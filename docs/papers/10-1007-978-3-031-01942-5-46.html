Keywords: Deep learning, Transfer learning, CNN, Handwriting, Parkinson’s disease

Parkinson’s disease (PD) is a degenerative and progressive disorder that affects the nervous system. The diagnosis of this disease remains a very important area of research since there is no subjective method of diagnosis used clinically. In this work, to address this problem, we involved a deep learning model using transfer learning from a pre-trained CNN called MobilNetV2 to classify patients with Parkinson’s disease. This model was retrained with a database of patients’ handwriting spirals. To remedy the problem of the poor size of our database, we used augmentation techniques consisting of some pre-processing layers (mirror effect, rotation, saturation…) to have enough images to train the model. These images include the pressure exerted on the screen when drawing spirals by representing each level of pressure with a specified color. The obtained results have proved very promising.
