Perceptions of Filipino Pre-service Teachers Toward the Quality of Online Learning During the COVID-19 Pandemic

Keywords: Online Learning, Quality of learning, Pandemic

The need to conduct studies probing appreciation and perception about online learning comes to the fore in this time when educational systems are slowly reverting to the traditional mode of teaching and learning. This is because digital education is the future. Its practice during the pandemic serves as a ‘pilot test’ for many countries. Thus, this study identified the different perceptions as well as factors that affected the perceived quality of online learning. The respondents of the study are preservice teachers who were exposed to both the traditional and modern modalities of learning. The empirical investigation employed a descriptive-qualitative design. It analyzed the respondents’ narratives on given questions. The participants were to vary their preference in terms of modality. Moreover, they were able to identify factors that impact the quality of their online learning experiences. Furthermore, the respondents are divided in their perceptions of whether there is quality or otherwise in online learning given the different constraints, struggles and limitations they experienced. The study discussion and implications on the different developed themes.
