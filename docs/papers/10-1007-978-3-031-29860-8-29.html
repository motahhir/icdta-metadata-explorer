A Novel Two-Port MIMO Configuration in Dual Band 26/38 GHz with High Isolation in the Ground Plane for 5G Applications

Keywords: 5G, ellipse antenna, mmWave, DGS, patch antenna, MIMO

The study of a new Multiple-Input-Multiple-Output (MIMO) antenna of two elements is presented with novel Defective Ground Structures (DGS) for isolation enhancement at the frequencies 26 GHz and 38 GHz for 5G and wireless applications. This MIMO consists of two identical elliptical monopole elements, and a new DGS for isolation increase in the ground plane’s bottom. To obtain very good performance we will first design the MIMO with two elements in several positions and second we will use Defective Ground Structures (DGS) for better isolation and miniaturization in the proposed structure provides good performance metrics such as gain, isolation, and total active reflection coefficient in the dual band from 25.988 GHz to 26.676 GHz and from 37.471 GHz to 38 GHz with peak gains 5.35 dBi at 26 GHz and 5.37 dBi at 38 GHz. Computer Simulation Technology-Microwave Studio (CST) has been used to study this antenna.
