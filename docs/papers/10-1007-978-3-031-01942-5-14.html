Keywords: Ethiopian enterprises, Technological innovation, Innovation

This article examines the findings of Ethiopia’s second national innovation survey, which took place in 2019. The conclusions of this study analyze the status of technological and non-technology innovations in Ethiopian firms, as well as indicate the hurdles to innovation. The findings of this study demonstrate that only 19.4% of enterprises were innovative, based on a list of 300,000 legally registered business-oriented corporations gathered from various government ministries and organizations. Product innovations were the most common followed by process innovations; around % of innovative businesses introduced technology improvements (product and/or process innovations). The inventive enterprises introduced 686 inventions, with 56% being product advances and 78.6% being technology improvements.
