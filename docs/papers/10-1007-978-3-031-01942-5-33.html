This paper is dedicated to the description of the procedure to be followed in the experimental validation of the fairness of the BEB (Binary Exponential Backoff) algorithm as well as the case of the presence of the bad behavior of this algorithm. We set up all the necessary tools for the experimentation, the generation and the analysis of communication traffic between the stations of our experimental bench. This work is conditioned by the availability of network cards that can support modifications to the protocols, including the BEB. The solution for this modification currently lies in the ATHEROS network cards (those which can support one of the three drivers: MADWIFI, ATH5K or ATH9K). The proposed experimental network consists of at least three stations (laptops) including a receiver and a cheat with integrated wireless cards. Laptops operate under IEEE 802.11. We put all the devices inside the lab to make sure they are under the same channel conditions and the only difference between legitimate and misbehaved nodes is the Back-off timer. JPerf, the commonly adopted network test tool, will be used to create traffic on the network, as well as generate User Datagram Protocol (UDP) streams that can fill the transmit queue on each device, putting all devices in full mode. Wireshark is employed to display the captured data and the Perl language to analyze the corresponding trace files.
