Balanced Scorecard as Intelligent Management Control Tools: Case of the Pharmaceutical Supply Chain of a University Hospital Center

Keywords: Balanced scorecard, Strategic dashboard, Intelligent management control, Hospital supply chain, Public hospital

Today, the performance and competitive advantage of organizations are seen globally, but they are achieved by controlling the activities that consume the most resources and that form the global map of company processes. In this respect, the competitive advantage comes more from the links between activities than from the activities themselves. Because of their role in linking the various activities within organizations, supply chains are reconsidered as functions of excellence for steering the performance of these organizations. Moreover, the Balanced Scorecard (BSC) is one of the tools that can be used by the hospital to ensure the automatic steering of the performance of its activities, more particularly the control of the hospital logistics chain. In this context, while carrying out action research at the level of a public hospital, our ambition in this study is to examine the role of the Balanced Scorecard in the management of hospital logistics performance in a new organizational context, and to evaluate the improvement that this combination of BSC and SCM could bring to the overall performance of hospitals.
