Keywords: Big Data, Blockchain, Higher Education, Data Management

Education is one of the key sectors that suffered significantly in this pandemic. With 35 million students enrolled in its higher educational institutions, India has been grappling with various technological challenges, such as effective data management - the issue of generating, handling, and securing millions of Gigabytes of information. The Education Information System needs a technological overhaul to face unprecedented times. The paper delivers a brief understanding of Big data and blockchain technology, followed by an observation of the Indian higher educational sector, the challenges, and if Blockchain could be a veritable solution for addressing these challenges. The ramifications of such a change and the scope for future research on Information systems are deliberate. This theoretical study investigates the impact blockchain technology could have on the higher education sector. The slow adoption of blockchain technology in education mirrors some serious problems, such as the lack of concrete incentives for blockchain mining. This paradox of weighing Blockchain's benefits with the issues in adopting the technology is critically discussed.
