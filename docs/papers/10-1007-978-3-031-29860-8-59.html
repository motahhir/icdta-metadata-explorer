Bearing Ball Fault Diagnosis of an Induction Machine by Using the Hilbert Transform and the Performance of Intelligent Control

Keywords: Artificial Neural Network-Direct Torque Control, Induction Machine, Bearing Faults, Diagnosis, Hilbert Transform

Electric machine defects are typically discovered using the Motor Current Signature Analysis (MCSA) technique, especially in Induction Motors (IMs). In the case of Bearing Faults (BFs), the identification and localization are closely linked to the rotational speed, therefore, the rotational frequency must be maintained at a specific reference value. Efficient control, such as Direct Torque Control (DTC), can be used to achieve this, but due to this control, both the current signal and the power frequency are nonstationary. It is mandatory to use sophisticated signal processing techniques. The Hilbert Transform (HT), which depends on BFs, is a useful tool for processing frequency content. In this paper the MCSA is adopted to detect the Ball defect of a bearing, based on the HT and the exploitation of an intelligent DTC based on Artificial Neural Networks (ANN-DTC) to preserve the rotation speed at a constant value. The results of the simulation showed the efficiency of the Hilbert filter for the detection of BFs. Additionally, the ANN-DTC command makes it possible to increase the amplitudes of the BF spectra by 30% compared to a conventional DTC. In MATLAB/SIMULINK, the ANN-DTC and diagnostic block are implemented.
