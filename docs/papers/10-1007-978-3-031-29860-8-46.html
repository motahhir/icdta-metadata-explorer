An Overview of the Impact of Partial Occlusions on Automatic Facial Expression Recognition

Keywords: Facial expressions, Facial occlusions, Recognition

Automatic facial expression recognition is useful in various areas, such as health, road safety and marketing, where feedback on user state is significant. Even with very good results in controlled settings (frontal face, no occlusion, good illumination), facial expression recognition is still challenging today under unconstrained environments (natural environment). Occlusions are particularly difficult to handle because they hide parts of the information on the images. Various solutions have been proposed to address this issue. These solutions can be generally categorized into two categories: those that focus on visible regions of the face and those that try to reconstruct the hidden part. Up-to-date solutions are mainly based on texture or geometry, and few are based on movement. In this paper, we show the impact of occlusions on facial expression recognition, the methods proposed to address them, and the protocols used in the literature to evaluate these methods.
