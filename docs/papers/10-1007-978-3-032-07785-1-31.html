Keywords: Atmospheric water generator, Water scarcity, Renewable energy, Thermoelectric cooling

Masses of people have been affected by the current worldwide water scarcity situation, especially in areas with poor access to fresh, clean water. The difficulty is addressed in this research through the design of an Atmospheric Water Generator (AWG) that uses thermoelectric materials and solar energy to draw water from humid air. Because of its scalable, sustainable, and affordable design, the system may be used both domestically and in agriculture in areas with limited water resources, like South Morocco. In order to contribute to sustainable development goals, the project uses cutting-edge digital technology and renewable energy integration to ensure a consistent supply of drinkable water. By an evaluation of research findings and a thorough literature review, this study outlines the AWG’s design, theoretical analysis, and possible impacts.
