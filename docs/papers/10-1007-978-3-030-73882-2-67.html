Financial institutions have been forced to make investments to maintain and improve customer satisfaction through E-banking offers. This electronic channel has demonstrated its importance by changing the traditional concept of financial institutions. By understanding the main factors that encourage customers to use electronic banking services, this helps to further develop this communication channel and take advantage of its benefits by allowing banks to remain competitive and profitable over the long term. This work is a state of the art which attempts to answer, through previous work in the field of E-banking, the following question: What are the factors influencing the adoption of electronic banking services among banking customers? Identifying these factors is an essential objective to change the characteristics of the service in order to make its adoption more attractive and increase its level of use. The results obtained confirm the presence of several recurring factors in the literature such as facilitating conditions, trust, perceived usefulness and social influence. By clearly identifying the most influential adoption factors, this enabled us to formulate a set of recommendations to the practitioners of E-banking.
