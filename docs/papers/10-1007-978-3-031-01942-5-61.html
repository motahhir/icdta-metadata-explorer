Effect of Number of PCM Tube Layers on the Dynamic and Thermal Behavior of a Cabinet Dryer

Keywords: CFD, PCM tubes, Indirect solar dryer

One of the biggest inherent problems with solar drying is the dependence on solar radiation. When the sun sets, the source of energy used for drying disappears, which increases the time required for drying as well as affects the quality of the products. To overcome this problem, a storage unit was introduced in an indirect cabinet dryer in the shape of copper tubes stuffed with gallium, a phase change material (PCM) that can store energy in the sun time and release it after sunset. This work contributes to the enhancement of the drying process by applying the CFD method, studying the effects of these tubes and the effect of multiple layers of them, as well as their inclination. To make this study more realistic, we used weather data from the city of Oujda (Eastern Morocco). The findings showed that as more layers of PCM tubes are used, more heat is stored at the time of sunshine, which significantly reduces the temperature of the chamber at day time, but when it comes to releasing this energy, the 2 layers showed the best performance. They release the energy in the same way as 3 layers, while for one layer, it barely releases anything. Regarding the inclination of these tubes, there is not much difference between vertical and inclined tubes, where the latter outperforms the vertical ones by about 1.6° at sun time.
