Keywords: Dolphin’s echolocation, NGD, Semantic similarity, SemantoSim, Shannon’s Entropy

A product recommendation system is the critical component of any functioning E-commerce industry. It is crucial for these corporations to present their application with a recommendation system capable of furnishing products according to the desires of their customers by considering an array of factors. This paper proposes a unique approach that integrates textual data and data comprised of images by hybridizing both Machine Learning and Deep Learning strategies, thereby emphasizing user search queries and product images impartially. Distinct stages are presented for intermediate image set generation prior to user search preferences and term set generation related to the user query. A customized web-crawler along with search queries and click rates are used respectively. The architecture utilizes a nature-inspired Dolphin’s echolocation metaheuristic algorithm, with objective functions scilicet SemantoSim, Normalized Google Distance (NGD), and Shannon’s entropy to compute semantic similarity and semantic deviants. Experimentations indicated when compared with baseline models that the proposed DF E-commerce outperformed the baseline models with an accuracy of 97.3% with an FDR of 0.04.
