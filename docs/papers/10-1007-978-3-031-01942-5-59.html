Inner Differential Current Suppression in Modular Multilevel Converter

Keywords: Modular multilevel converter (MMC), Inner differential current, Capacitor voltage ripples

The applicability of the modular multilevel converter is expanding in diverse industrial applications. The presence of inner differential current can affect the performances and efficiency of this converter, increase the conduction losses, and add more ripples in capacitor voltages. This paper compares two control methods to suppress the AC component of the inner differential currents in each phase. The first uses a controller based on a DQ synchronous reference frame, while the second is based upon a moving average filter (MAF). The inverter structure of the MMC is modeled and simulated in the PLECS environment to evaluate the impact of inner differential current on capacitor voltage ripples and arm currents.
