Fano Resonance in a Defective Network Formed by Helmholtz Resonators with Extended Necks

Keywords: Acoustic attenuation, Defects, Acoustic band gaps, Fano resonance

The Helmholtz resonator has been extensively used to attenuate narrowband low-frequency noise in a variety of industrial applications. Theoretical and experimental studies have been carried out to improve the acoustic performance of Helmholtz resonators (HRs), particularly at low frequencies. The Helmholtz resonator is simple, reliable, and low cost, which gives it an advantage compared with other resonators. However, achieving significant noise attenuation needs these devices to be precisely tuned. Additionally, when the space is limited, increasing HR dimensions to achieve more attenuation is no longer possible, which adds another limitation of the use of this type of resonator. Thus, Helmholtz resonators with an extended neck (HREN) and fixed cavity volume can be an effective solution. In this work, the acoustic attenuation of HRENs is predicted by a corrected one-dimensional (1D) analytical approach. This modified 1D approach is based on 2D analytical results introduced to consider nonplanar effects caused by the extended length. The theoretical results obtained by the transfer matrix, and Green’s function, fit well with numerical simulations and experiments. We also take advantage of the periodicity to produce acoustic band gaps using periodic HRENs in which two defects are introduced inside the structure. In this study, defects are in the form of two closed resonators, connected one in front of the other, to study the characteristics of the selected mode inside band gaps. We show that the acoustic coupling of defect modes gives rise to Fano resonance inside the band gap. The defective cell can have potential applications in noise control at low frequencies.
