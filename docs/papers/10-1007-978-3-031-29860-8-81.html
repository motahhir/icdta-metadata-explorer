A Genetic-Based Algorithm for Commodity Selection and Full Truckload Vehicle Routing Problem

Keywords: Full truckload, Order selection, Time windows, MILP, Genetic algorithm

In this paper, an order selection and full truckload routing problem is studied with the goal of maximizing the total profit generated by the trucks throughout their return journeys while meeting the constraints of time windows. The problem is decomposed into two parts: selecting a subset of commodities to serve and finding an optimal sequence of commodities assigned to each truck. For this problem, a mixed-integer linear programming (MILP) formulation is presented, which turned out to be NP-hard to solve due to its complexity. To solve the model, a cooperative metaheuristic algorithm is proposed, in which a genetic algorithm (GA) is employed to obtain feasible routes and the MILP model is used to find the best optimal sequence of commodities for each truck route. The two strategy components are seamlessly integrated, and the best solution is discovered after iterating through the routes in search of improvement. Simulations are performed using a randomly generated dataset, and the results are evaluated for performance against the CPLEX solver. It was found that the proposed solution procedure outperforms the solver in terms of solution quality and CPU time and outperforms the GA in terms of solution quality.
